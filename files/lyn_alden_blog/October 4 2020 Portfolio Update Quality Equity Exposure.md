# Portfolio Update: Quality Equity Exposure

*October 4, 2020*

![Premium Feature Image](https://www.lynalden.com/wp-content/uploads/premium-feature.jpg)

This is a quick note between normal reports to update readers about upcoming portfolio moves.

Back on [June 9](https://www.lynalden.com/risk-mitigation-update/), I de-risked the newsletter portfolio slightly by trimming a bit of equity exposure and putting it into Treasuries. This was due to unusual skews in the put/call ratio, overbought conditions, and market euphoria more generally with the prospect of stimulus running out in the months ahead.

All major equity indices corrected and consolidated after that. After the consolidation, though, mega-cap tech stocks were able to push to new highs and bring the S&P 500 with them, while broader measures like the equal-weight S&P 500, and small cap indices, mostly remained in a sideways pattern for a while. Here’s the equal-weight S&P 500 vs our June 9th slight risk-off move:

![RSP Technical](https://www.lynalden.com/wp-content/uploads/premium-2020-10-4-rsp-technical.png)

In September, we further pruned the portfolio by selling some of the high-flying winners from the past year or two like NVDA, ADBE, and PYPL (something I’m hesitant to do, but they got too stretched), and rotating into some other growth stocks like BABA, JD, FB. We also did similar pruning/rotations within the dividend stock section.

Now, we’re in a difficult phase. We’re in disinflation/defense environment in the near-term, subject to stimulus or lack thereof, but within a [longer-term macro outlook](https://www.lynalden.com/premium-themes-2020-8-2/) for inflation and currency devaluation, based on large monetized fiscal deficits. The upcoming election, which is likely to be contested at first, throws a big volatility wrench into this quarter as well.

However, geographic diversification and an emphasis on appropriately-valued companies can mitigate the risk somewhat. In the [September 27 report](https://www.lynalden.com/premium-2020-9-27/), I announced a very small risk-on move to be made on September 28th for the newsletter portfolio (shifting 2% from Treasuries into the growth stock pie), which was completed.

Stimulus talks are getting closer. Democrats passed a $2.2 trillion bill, and the White House has countered with $1.6 trillion. McConnell is fine with the $1.6 trillion proposal, and this is now close enough for potential compromise (months ago, it was $3 trillion vs $1 trillion). McConnell, Pelosi, and Mnuchin all indicated that a deal could very well happen soon.

If anything, it’s likely more of a 2-vs-1 debate because Mnuchin, on behalf of Trump’s White House, wants a stimulus prior to the election too. If anything, it would make tactical election sense for Trump and his team to err on the side of going big on pre-election stimulus, and securing a compromise somewhere around $1.8-$2 trillion. However, the Senate has more fiscal conservatives in it, so out of the three groups, that one has been the more resistant group to the larger stimulus numbers.

Investors can decide for themselves whether to front-run the potential stimulus, or wait to see if it actually happens, since talks could still easily fail. Each investor has their own risk tolerance, time horizon, and financial goals. After this consolidation phase, the president’s COVID-19 hospitalization, and closer stimulus talks, I’m erring on the side of quality equity exposure, with a 3-5 year outlook for the companies I own.
