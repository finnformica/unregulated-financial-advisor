# Trimming into Strength

*September 9, 2020*

![Premium Feature Image](https://www.lynalden.com/wp-content/uploads/premium-feature.jpg)

This brief lunchtime note provides some extra updates for recent volatile market conditions.

I recently sent out an extra report on August 23rd, highlighting some heightened risks in the market, due to the recent surge in tech equity valuations, and a lack of stimulus bill that would justify some of the equity mania. And then in the normally-scheduled August 30th premium report, I re-iterated some of those observations.

The market has been very volatile over the past week, with many tech names in particular selling off hard. Today, however, there is a big bounce, with many tech names up big. It provides an opportunity to consider taking profits on some richly-valued positions.

Each investor has their own unique situation, risk tolerance, and investment goals, but I would suggest taking this big positive day in the market to look through your portfolio and ensure that your allocations are appropriate.

Although I don’t particularly like to sell winners too often, I am selling NVDA from the growth stocks section of the newsletter portfolio. This one was added in July 2019 at under $170/share and then dollar-cost averaged into for a while, and as of today is well over $500/share.

That’s almost a 3x gain from the entry price in 14 months, and the stock now has a price/sales ratio of 24x with a market cap of over $300B.

When I added it, its price/sales ratio was 9x and its market cap was a bit over $100B, so it was an expensive but high-quality growth stock that I was willing to buy on weakness. From there it went straight up like a rocket ship, mainly due to rising valuations:

![NVDA FAST Graph](https://www.lynalden.com/wp-content/uploads/premium-2020-9-nvda-fastgraph.png)

*Chart Source: F.A.S.T. Graphs*

In addition, in the newsletter portfolio, I am trimming the overall allocation to the growth stocks pie from 8% to 7%, and trimming the small-cap value ETF (SLYV) from 5% to 4%, and adding those 2% to SHY, the short-term Treasury ETF.

The ETF-only portfolio is also trimming SLYV by -1% to add +1% to SHY.

This upcoming Sunday evening will have a normally-scheduled full premium report.
