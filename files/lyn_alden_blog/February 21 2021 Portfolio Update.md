# Portfolio Update

*February 21, 2021*

![Premium Feature Image](https://www.lynalden.com/wp-content/uploads/premium-feature.jpg)

This is a brief note between normal reports. The next normally-scheduled premium report comes out next week.

I published a free public newsletter today (which I do every six weeks), and wanted to make sure premium members saw it too:

* [February 2021 Newsletter: The Yield Stress Point](https://www.lynalden.com/february-2021-newsletter/)

In addition, I’m making a few portfolio updates next week:

**Newsletter Portfolio**

Sell the 7% stake in the T-bill ETF (BIL), and buy a 7% stake in the Quadratic Interest Rate Volatility ETF (IVOL). IVOL is a nice hedge against a steepening yield curve.

**Fortress Income Portfolio**

Sell Texas Pacific (TPL) and Lam Research (LRCX) after their massive run-ups (huge performance). Trim HDFC Bank (HDB) and BlackRock (BLK) each from 4% to 3% in the individual stock pie after their big gains.

Buy a 3% stake in Chevron (CVX), a 3% stake in Bank of America (BAC), and a 3% stake in Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) in the individual stock pie.

Sell the 30% T-bill ETF (BIL) slot in the Defense section, and buy the Quadratic Interest Rate Volatility ETF (IVOL) in its place.

**ETF Portfolio**

Reduce the iShares 1-3 Year Treasury ETF (SHY) from 14% to 6%, and reduce the gold ETF (AAAU) from 8% to 7%.

Buy 7% of the Quadratic Interest Rate Volatility ETF (IVOL), buy 2% of the S&P Regional Banking ETF (KRE).

**No Limits Portfolio**

-No changes.

**Other Holdings**

In yen on the Tokyo Stock Exchange, I’ll buy Mitsui (8031), Mitsubishi (8058), and Hitachi (6501) with the intention of them being long-term holds. I already have a sizable Itochu ADR (ITOCY) position.

Readers know that I’m bullish on the various Japanese trading companies and on Hitachi and have written about a number of them in previous reports, with the general expectation of the 2020s decade being a decent one for commodities and industrials, at a time when they’ve improved corporate governance and deleveraged their balance sheets.

Secondly, I’m selling my position in Cheesecake Factory (CAKE). I’m not exactly bearish on it; I just don’t have any conviction about it relative to a broad index now.

I initially recommended it in the high-$30s/share [in a December 2019 report](https://www.lynalden.com/premium-2019-12-8/), which of course ended up being terrible timing in hindsight, due to the black swan pandemic a few months later that shut down virtually every one of their restaurants.

Then in a [March 2020 report](https://www.lynalden.com/premium-2020-3-5/), as everyone was running away from the stock market, I discussed the plan to sell cash-secured puts on some stocks including CAKE as it crashed in price. Those puts were assigned, and the cost basis those shares were acquired at in summer 2020 was a little over $23/share.

The stock is now over $50/share.

![CAKE FASTGraph](https://www.lynalden.com/wp-content/uploads/premium-2021-2-21-cake.png)

*Chart Source: F.A.S.T. Graph*

So, we’ve ironically made out like bandits on an unprofitable restaurant stock during a pandemic, with approximately 35% price gains from the initial recommendation and 125% price gains from the second tranche. Be greedy when others are fearful, as they say.

Now that the market is greedy, I don’t mind taking profits here. I’ll wait and see what things look like next year, since I don’t have much of a way to forecast their 2021-2022 potential and it feels too much like guessing. I’ll put this one in the “too hard pile” for now and take the win.

I do think the long-term potential of their restaurant brands, particularly North Italia as well as their portfolio of small brands, are likely to do well in the decade ahead. However, the stock is more expensive and less profitable than when I initially analyzed it, which gives pause for concern.
